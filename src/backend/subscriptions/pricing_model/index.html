Credits instead of AI caps proposed

Drop the per-tool AI limits — each plan just includes a monthly credit balance
replaces "1 OneClick run · Zoe/Registry off"
replaces "unlimited AI" — and caps your COGS
PlanCredits / mo≈ OneClick runs≈ Zoe messages≈ Registry parsesMax OpenAI COGS

Under this model AI tools have no separate on/off or run cap — the credit balance is the limit, and running out just pauses AI until next period (or a top-up). It also caps your runaway AI spend — today Pro is "unlimited", so one power user's OneClick loop is uncapped COGS. Pair it with dropping the zero-cost paywalls (projects, tasks, split-sheets, artists) — flip the tool cards to Proposed — and the only things you meter are AI (credits) and storage (GB): the two things that actually cost you money.

Notes & levers

✓ shipped Unified parse cache

OneClick + Registry share one canonical-text cache. The extraction call is served from cache on repeat/cross-tool runs, so a warm OneClick run drops 3→2 gpt-5.2 calls. Drag cache hit rate to see the blend.

next win Cache/downgrade the advisory passes

With extraction cached, basis + split-verify are the whole warm-run bill — 2 gpt-5.2 calls that are log-only/advisory. Cache or downgrade them and a warm OneClick approaches free.

to build Credits ledger replaces AI caps

Today AI is gated by on/off flags + per-period counters. The proposed model swaps those for a credits balance the AI tools debit per use — no more `oneclick_enabled` / `max_oneclick_runs`. Needs a ledger column + measured response.usage so debits reflect real spend, not estimates.

gap No storage overage today

Pro storage is -1 (unlimited) — a hard binary cap on Free (1 GB), nothing metered on Pro. The economics above are a proposal to recover Supabase's ~$0.0213/GB above 100 GB.
