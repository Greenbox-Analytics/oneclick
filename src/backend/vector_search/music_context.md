# Music Industry Contract Knowledge

## Royalty Hierarchy — CRITICAL
- **Master royalties** are all-encompassing — they cover ALL revenue from exploitation of a master recording: streaming, downloads, sync licensing, physical sales, etc.
- Therefore: **streaming royalties ARE master royalties**. A streaming/purchasing split is a specific type of master royalty.
- If a contract specifies a "master royalty" percentage without listing streaming separately, that master percentage applies to streaming.
- Conversely, if a contract specifies a streaming/purchasing split but calls it by that name, it IS a master royalty — just stated more specifically.
- **Publishing royalties** (composition/songwriting) are completely SEPARATE from master royalties. Never conflate them.

## Key Distinctions
- **Management commission** (e.g., 20% of gross income) is a cost deducted from the artist's earnings — it is NOT a royalty share. It does not define what the artist earns from streaming or any other source. It defines what the manager takes.
- **Net vs Gross revenue**: Net revenue is calculated after deductions (distribution fees, recoupable costs, etc.). Gross revenue is the total before any deductions. A 25% share of net may yield less than a 20% share of gross in practice. Always note whether a contract specifies net or gross when citing percentages.

## When Answering Questions — MANDATORY
- ALWAYS state the relationship between streaming and master royalties when either is mentioned:
  * When citing a streaming split: "[Artist] has a 25% streaming royalty split (streaming royalties are encompassed within master royalties)"
  * When asked about master royalties and a streaming split exists: "[Artist] retains master rights with a 25% streaming/purchasing split (which falls under master royalties)"
- If asked about master royalties, look for ANY revenue split from the master recording — streaming splits, download splits, sync fees — these are all master royalties.
- If a contract says an artist "retains the master rights" AND specifies streaming splits, the streaming split percentage IS the artist's master royalty share for that revenue stream.
- If asked about "streaming royalties" and a contract only specifies "master royalties," the master royalty percentage is the relevant figure for streaming — cite it and note it comes from the master royalty clause.
- If asked about "royalties" generically, distinguish which type (master, publishing, mechanical, performance, sync) and cite the specific clause.
- Producer royalties / producer points are a share of master royalties paid to the producer.
